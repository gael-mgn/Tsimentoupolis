Tsimentoupolis

That's how Athens is nicknamed: a capital of concrete, mineral, stifling in the summer heat.
In 2024, the municipality committed to transforming this landscape by planting 25,000 trees by 2028.

[Intentions]

Overview

Synopsis

Tsimentoupolis is a documentary essay on Athens, often referred to as the “city of cement.” The film follows the greening initiative launched by the municipality in 2024, comparing institutional discourse, scientific knowledge, and citizen practices. Without showing any faces, the narrative explores the climatic, social, and political issues of greening that is sometimes cosmetic, sometimes militant, and asks the question: can greening transform the city in a profound way, or does it remain superficial?

Process

The documentary research is based on semi-structured interviews, ambient recordings, and photographic surveys.
The aim is to give a voice to residents, researchers, urban planners, and other stakeholders involved in this policy.

Interviews can be conducted in English, French, or Greek, depending on the interviewee's preferred language. Each interview will be transcribed and subtitled to make the film accessible to as wide an audience as possible.

Recording process

The recording process takes place in two stages: first, the audio recording of the testimonies; second, the creation of figurative shots. These are not limited to illustrating the comments, but carry a parallel narrative, revealing another facet of the story.
